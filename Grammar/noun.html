Nouns

The simplest definition of a noun is a thing and nouns are the basic building blocks of sentences. These things can represent a person, animal, place, idea, emotion â€“ almost any thing that you can think of.
Dog, Sam, love, phone, Chicago, courage and spaceship are all nouns.

The cat chased a mouse across the garden under the moonlight .
I love watching my cat play with the pink yarn .
It is raining! Everyone , grab your umbrella and rain hat and watch out for the puddles!

Types of Nouns

Proper Nouns :

Nouns tha t are used to name a person, place or thing specifically are called a proper noun. Proper nouns always begin with a capital letter.

  1. Alice visited the Eiffel Tower in Paris during her vacation.
  2. Amazon plans to open a new distribution center in Los Angeles next year.
  3. Lionel Messi signed a new contract with Inter Miami in July 2023.

Common Nouns :

Common nouns are those nouns that refer to a generic item, group or place. This means that, unlike proper nouns, they are not used to identify specific people, places or objects. Common nouns are not capitalised unless they appear at the beginning of a sentence.

  1. I bought a pen yesterday. (Common object)
  2. I am going to school . (Common place)
  3. Only ten employees showed up to work today. (Common group)

Concrete Nouns :

Concrete nouns refer to specific items, people, or places that are tangible and can be counted. Concrete nouns are always capitalised.

  1. The artist used vibrant colors to paint a stunning landscape .
  2. The person threw the rock across the yard .
  3. The chef prepared a delicious meal in the kitchen .

Abstract Nouns :

Any entity that cannot be perceived by the five senses of the human body are called an abstract noun. Abstract nouns are nouns that do not have a specific physical form or that are not directly observable. Abstract nouns are always in lowercase.

  1. Love and friendship are equally important.
  2. Honesty is the best policy.
  3. It takes a lot of courage to raise your voice and stand up against injustice.

Collective Nouns :

Collective nouns are nouns that refer to a group of people or things. They are always in lowercase and do not have articles or possessive forms.

  1. Collective nouns for groups of animals :
    • A pride of lions
    • A flock of sheep
    • A swarm of bees
  2. Collective nouns for groups of people :
    • A band of musicians
    • A board of directors
    • A crew of sailors
  3. Collective nouns for a number of things/objects :
    • A pair of shoes
    • A chain of mountains
    • A bunch of grapes
